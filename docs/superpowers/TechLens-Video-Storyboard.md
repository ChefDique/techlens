# TechLens — Demo Video Shot List & Storyboard

**Runtime Target:** 3:30–3:50 (hard cap: 4:00)
**Setting:** 1950s auto shop aesthetic, 2026 technology
**Tone:** Authentic, funny, human — then pivot to real product
**Cast:** Richard (young, tech-forward mechanic), Old Tech (frustrated veteran), Service Writer (dramatic, fed up), Off-screen voice (co-worker)

---

## SCENE 1 — "THE FUMBLE"
**Purpose:** Viral hook. Immediate relatability. No dialogue needed.
**Duration:** 0:00–0:12

| Shot | Type | Visual | Audio | Duration |
|------|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 1A | Wide | Under-hood of car. Greasy hands reach for phone sitting on fender. | Shop ambience, metal clanking | 2s |
| 1B | Close-up | Phone slipping between greasy fingers, fumbled back and forth, nearly dropped | Phone tapping against skin | 3s |
| 1C | Quick zoom | Tech catches phone, taps camera button, takes accidental photo of ceiling | Camera shutter sound | 2s |
| 1D | Medium | Old tech pushes reading glasses up nose, holds phone at arm's length, squinting hard | One long sigh | 3s |
| 1E | Extreme close-up | Greasy fingertip pressing screen button repeatedly. Screen glitches, won't register touch. Wet-touch errors. | Rapid tap-tap-tap-tap | 2s |

**Image/video gen direction:**
- 1950s-styled mechanic: classic grey/blue work shirt with oval name patch, reading glasses perched on nose tip
- Hands covered in dark grease, knuckles cracked
- Phone is modern but the man is analog
- Shop background: vintage pin-up tool calendar on wall, but modern hydraulic lift visible

## SCENE 3 — "THE ARGUMENT"
**Purpose:** The communication breakdown. The core problem statement.
**Duration:** 0:22–0:45

| Shot | Type | Visual | Audio | Duration |
|------|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 3A | Medium | Service writer storms into frame from the right, waving a printed repair order | Footsteps, paper flapping | 3s |
| 3B | Over-shoulder (tech POV) | Writer holds up the RO, pointing at handwritten notes | Writer: "What in the world does this even say?! And what does this mean? Look at this — does that say milliamp? What the heck is an ohm?!" | 5s |
| 3C | Reverse (writer POV) | Tech fires back, gesturing at the computer and the manuals behind him | Tech: "You don't understand. First of all, I hate typing. And translating my sophisticated language into human-speak is YOUR job. First I gotta log into this computer, then I gotta log into that system, then I gotta log into this thing, then I gotta click on this to get to that — 30 minutes later I haven't even started diagnosing the car, and you walk up to me saying my notes aren't good enough?" | 10s |
| 3D | Wide | Both still arguing. Camera slowly pulls back. Voices fade to muffle. Shop noise takes over. | Muffled argument fading under ambient shop sounds | 4s |
| 3E | Same wide shot | Optional text overlay as camera settles: **"Every shop. Every day. Every car."** | Ambient only | 2s |

**Image/video gen direction:**
- Service writer: button-up shirt with dealership logo, khakis, holding a crumpled RO with illegible handwriting
- Both gesturing dramatically — 1950s screwball comedy energy
- The printed RO should be visible enough to see it's genuinely messy/unreadable

**Dialogue notes:**
- Tech's rant is the money speech. Deliver it fast, building frustration. Not yelling — exasperated.
- Writer is theatrical. He's done. He can't take it anymore.

## SCENE 5 — "YO GEMMY"
**Purpose:** The agent in action. Voice-first. Natural. Interruptible.
**Duration:** 1:05–1:40

| Shot | Type | Visual | Audio | Duration |
|------|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 5A | Medium | Richard cups his hands and calls out across the bay: "Yo Gemmy!" | Richard: "Yo Gemmy!" — casual, like calling a friend | 2s |
| 5B | Cut to screen | Animated TechLens/Gemini logo on screen. Reactive audio waveform pulses in response. | Gemmy: "Yes Richard, I'm here." | 3s |
| 5C | Close-up Richard | Richard, mid-thought, slightly stumbling: "I've got a, uhh — what did you say the year and model was?" | Natural, not rehearsed delivery | 3s |
| 5D | Motion graphic overlay | TechLens logo with chat bubble pops up on screen: **"Natural conversational flow... including interruptions."** | Subtle UI chime | 3s |
| 5E | Wide two-shot | Man shouts the info from across the shop. Richard relays: "2023 Outback, CVT issues — is there a new TSB?" | Shouted casually across the shop | 4s |
| 5F | Screen + audio | Gemmy's waveform animates as it responds | Gemmy: "Yep. TSB 02-157-22R addresses customer concerns of transmission slippage and includes a 35-page diagnosis tree. Ready to begin?" | 6s |
| 5G | Close-up Richard | Deadpan: "No. I need to take a leak." | Flat delivery. Beat. | 2s |
| 5H | Screen | Gemmy waveform pulses. Pause. Then: | Gemmy: "Would you like the diagnosis flow chart for leak diagnosis?" | 3s |
| 5I | Motion graphic overlay | TechLens logo with chat bubble: **"Context-aware. Even when you wish it wasn't."** | Comedic beat. Laugh moment. | 3s |

**Dialogue notes:**
- 5C must sound exactly like someone mid-conversation fumbling a thought. Not scripted.
- 5G-5H is the comedic peak of the video. Timing is everything. Gemmy's response needs a half-second pause before delivering.
- 5F: use a real TSB number from the knowledge base (02-157-22R is in our data)

| 6C | Close-up Richard | Richard reaches up and clicks on a flashlight mounted to his goofy webcam eyewear rig | Flashlight click sound | 3s |
| 6D | Close-up Richard | Richard, looking up at the car: "Yo Gemmy, look at this!" | Casual command | 2s |

| 6G | Two-shot under car | Richard: "Well, that's a bust." Other tech: "Yeah, but at least we weren't wasting a bunch of time trying to figure out if we had the right tool or if we even have the tool." | Genuine, relieved tone | 5s |
| 6H | Screen | Gemmy waveform | Gemmy: "That's very true. But if you show me your special tools, I could possibly tell you which one it is — if you do have it." | 4s |

- The webcam feed should show realistic under-car components with a subtle TechLens UI overlay identifying what it sees
- Lighting: flashlight-lit from below, dramatic shadows on car undercarriage

## SCENE 7 — "THE WRAP-UP"
**Purpose:** The three-output payoff. This is the feature that wins the hackathon.
**Duration:** 2:15–2:45

| Shot | Type | Visual | Audio | Duration |
|------|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 7A | Screen + wide | Gemmy's waveform. Richard and other tech walking out from under the car, wiping hands. | Gemmy: "Alright. I'm going to summarize the steps we took today and note the important details — especially that you don't have the tool to finish the job." | 6s |
| 7B | Screen UI | Three documents animate into view side by side: **TECH NOTES** / **CUSTOMER SUMMARY** / **ESCALATION BRIEF** | Subtle animation sound as each card appears. One. Two. Three. | 5s |
| 7C | Motion graphic | TechLens logo with text: **"One session. Three outputs. Tech notes. Customer summary. Escalation brief."** | Clean branded audio sting | 4s |
| 7D | Screen + medium | Gemmy waveform. Richard grabbing his bag. | Gemmy: "I've added it to the repair order. Don't forget — two more complaint lines to address tomorrow. Get some rest!" | 5s |
| 7E | Medium | Richard pulls off his goofy eyewear, smiles, walks toward the shop exit. Other tech glances at the screen with the three documents, nods approvingly. | Shop winding down. Lights clicking off. | 5s |
| 7F | Quick cut | Flash to: old tech from Scene 1, still hunt-and-pecking at his keyboard with one greasy finger. | Single keyboard tap. | 3s |

**Image/video gen direction:**
- 7B is THE money shot of the entire video. Three clean document cards, each visibly different:
  - Tech Notes: structured, bullet points, part numbers, clinical
  - Customer Summary: plain English, short paragraphs, no jargon
  - Escalation Brief: formal, detailed measurements, DTCs, diagnostic steps
- 7F callback is the punchline button on the whole narrative

| Shot | Type | Visual | Audio | Duration |

| 8A | Cut to black | Half-second black screen. Palette shift. We're out of the 1950s now. | Silence | 1s |
| 8B | Screen recording | Real TechLens app running: voice input going in, agent responding in real time, camera feed active | Richard V/O: "TechLens is real. Here's the actual product." | 8s |
| 8C | Screen recording | Show: session starting with vehicle selection, voice conversation flowing, tool calls happening | Richard V/O: "One voice session replaces 30 minutes of typing, three logins, and a stack of manuals." | 8s |
| 8D | Graphic | Architecture diagram appears: React → Cloud Run → Gemini Live API → Firestore | Richard V/O: "Built on Google's Gemini Live API and Agent Development Kit, deployed on Cloud Run with Firestore." | 6s |
| 8E | Screen recording | Show the three output documents generating from a real session | Richard V/O: "Every session automatically generates tech notes, a customer summary, and an escalation brief." | 6s |
| 8F | Direct to camera or text card | Richard (real, not character): "I spent years as the translator between techs, writers, customers, and manufacturer reps. TechLens does that job automatically." | Direct delivery, personal, authentic | 6s |

**Production notes:**
- This section MUST be real screen capture of the working product
- The transition from cinematic to screen-capture should feel intentional — we told you the story, now we're showing you the proof
- Architecture diagram should be clean, minimal, with Google service logos visible
